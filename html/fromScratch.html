Step by Step Tutorial For Fall Decor Pumpkin

Needed Materials

  1. Super Bulky Weight #6 Yarn. (I always use YarnBee)
    • I always use YarnBee Brand
  2. 8mm Crochet Hook
    • Amazon has some great sets of needles
  3. Tapestry Needle
    • Find a set that includes tapestry needles. If not, any craft store will have cheap ones.
  4. Scissors
    • There are special yarn cutter to concider so you can travel with them.
  5. Cinnamon Sticks
    • These are the stems of the pumpkins. Found at any craft store.
  6. Polyester Fiberfill
    • I got a big 5lb box at Hobby Lobby on sale for pretty cheap.
  7. Twine
    • Can be found very cheap at Walmart or any craft store.

      Let's Get Started - Step #1

      Go ahead and make a starting casting loop. This is done by making a loop in the yarn and pulling one strand through it. Tighten the loop around your yarn hook.

      Start your first row! We are going to chain 16. One chain is when you yarn over your hook from the back to the front and pull that new loop through the previous loop. After doing this 16 times, we'll start working in the opposite direction.

      Count your chains by counting how many V's you have made. Reference the picture below.

      Let's Keep Going - Step #2

      Once you finish chaining 16, we're going to turn our work and start doing half-double crochet stitches (hdc).

      Continue your hdc's for the whole row (row 2). After you have done all of them, you should have 14 stitches.

      Chain two more stitches and turn your work. For this project, we will be working in the back loop only.

      This is what your project should look like up to this point.

      Sewing Your Pumpkin - Step #3

      Take the yarn ends that you have at the beginning and end of your square and thread them through the tapestry needle. Weave the needle through loops that are close together so the ends aren't visible anymore.

      Now we are going to fold our project in half and sew the open side using a slip stitch. Cast the yarn onto the hook like we did in the beginning.

      Your project should be a cylander shape now. Weave in the ends again when you're done.

      Finishing off your Pumpkin - Step #4

      To finish off our pumpking we are going to thread the end of your yarn through the tapestry needle. Poke the needle through loops along the top opening of your cylander. It doesn't matter what direction or what loops it is, just do a lot of them as this will be pulled tight. Once you have gone all the way around and have pulled the ends tight, cut the yarn and tie the ends together.

      Now that our pumpkin is a bowl shape, we are going to fill it with a lot of stuffing. It should be enough that the pumpkin is stiff and holds it's shape, but not so much that is is weirdly lumpy or unable to close.

      Repeat the same process of sewing the open edge with the tapestry needle. Insert a cinnamon stick into the opening before tieing off the yarn. The cinnamon stick will act as the pumpkin stem. Take our the cinnamon stick and wrap yarn around pumpkin to add bumps in the pumpkin. I used three loops to create 6 bumps in the pumpkin. Add the cinnamon stick back in. Style it further by tying a bow with some onto the base of the cinnamon stick stem.

      Refer to this YouTube video for any additional instructions or if you are a visual learner. Enjoy your fall pumpkin that will look like you bought at the store!
